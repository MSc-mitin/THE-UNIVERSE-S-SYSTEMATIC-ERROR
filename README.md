## Abstract

This work proposes a thermodynamic theory of level-emergence. Its core is a single causal pattern — gradient → retention → pole → transition — that governs every step of complexification, from dissipative structures upward. Each step is constituted by a pole: a thermodynamically selected distinction, retention on one side of which defines the level. The sequence is ordered by expanding scale of localization, which makes it predictive rather than descriptive. Complexity is reframed not as what resists the second law but as its instrument: the most effective way to dissipate a gradient is to build something that resists its own dissipation. Consciousness, in this picture, is one branch of the structure (the 3→4 transition), not the subject of the theory as a whole. The theory carries a falsifiable predicate: the form of a vertical transition is forced and an alternative form is impossible.
